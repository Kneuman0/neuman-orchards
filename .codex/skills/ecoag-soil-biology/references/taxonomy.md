## Major Themes

### 1. Compost tea production, application, and outcomes

- Production variables: compost quality, feedstock, extraction method, aerated vs. non-aerated systems, dechlorinated water, filtration, dilution.
- Application variables: foliar vs. root application, irrigation compatibility, crop-specific dose sensitivity.
- Claimed effects: growth support, physiological response, partial fertilizer reduction, disease suppression claims.
- Main source: `Pane-et-al-2025-compost-tea-review.pdf`

### 2. Rhizosphere mechanisms for plant nutrition

- Plant-microbe interaction frame: microbiome assembly, root exudates, rhizosphere adaptation.
- Nutrition mechanisms: microbial effects on nutrient bioavailability, especially N, P, S, Fe, K, Zn.
- Mechanistic boundaries: what is known for mineral nutrition vs. what remains unclear about microbiome control.
- Main source: `Jacoby-et-al-2017-soil-microorganisms-plant-nutrition.pdf`

### 3. Inoculant formulation and delivery technology

- Product design: carrier choice, liquid vs. organic vs. inorganic vs. polymeric vs. encapsulated formulations.
- Deployment logistics: seed coating, soil application, bulk sterilization, mass culture, shelf-life, moisture sensitivity.
- Failure modes: poor carrier choice, rapid population decline after application, mismatch between isolate and delivery method.
- Main source: `Bashan-et-al-2013-pgpr-inoculant-technology.pdf`

### 4. Domain boundaries and handoffs

- Scope anchor: `references/expert-brief.md`
- Use this brief first for mission, handoffs, and output contract.
- Hand off to compost/fertility when the real question is base organic matter quality; to fungi/biostimulants for mycorrhiza-dominant questions; to pest ecology for disease-resistance or suppression questions that exceed this folder's evidence.

## Weak Evidence And Gaps

- Compost tea results are heterogeneous across feedstocks, compost quality, extraction parameters, crop species, and dose; this folder does not support one universal brewing protocol.
- Pane states that the biostimulatory mechanisms behind compost tea are not completely understood, even where positive crop responses are reported.
- Disease-suppression claims exist in Pane, but this folder is thinner on robust decision rules for diagnosing whether a real-world problem is pathogen pressure, nutrient imbalance, or broader stress ecology.
- Jacoby gives strong mechanism framing for nutrient mobilization but also states that microbiome assembly mechanisms and root-exudate-driven shifts remain largely unknown.
- Bashan is strong on inoculant technology through 2013, but this folder is weak on newer commercial formulations and modern field-validation data after that review window.
- This folder is weak on crop-specific Mid-Atlantic timing, farm-scale economics, and mature multi-domain prescriptions; those require handoff or synthesis with neighboring experts.
