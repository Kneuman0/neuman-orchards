# Fungal Ecology Retrieval Taxonomy

Use this file to route questions to the fastest relevant local source before answering. Start here, then open the exact source file(s) named below.

## Major Themes

### 1. AMF symbiosis and plant-growth regulation
- Core mechanisms: root colonization, extra-radical hyphae, common mycorrhizal networks, nutrient exchange, photosynthesis support, water relations.
- Soil-function subthemes: glomalin-related soil protein, aggregation, water holding, nutrient access, reduced dependence on synthetic fertilizer.
- Primary source: `knowledgebase/07_fungi_mycorrhiza_biostimulants/Begum-et-al-2019-amf-growth-regulation-review.pdf`

### 2. AMF-mediated abiotic stress tolerance
- Drought: water uptake, water-use efficiency, osmotic adjustment, antioxidant activity, ABA and related stress physiology.
- Salinity: ionic balance, reduced Na uptake, osmolytes, phytohormones, photosynthesis protection.
- Heavy metals: immobilization, sequestration, chelation, glomalin binding, reduced translocation.
- Temperature stress: heat and cold tolerance, chlorophyll protection, metabolite shifts, water relations.
- Combined stresses: broad mechanism overview exists, but the paper explicitly notes the evidence base is thin.
- Primary source: `knowledgebase/07_fungi_mycorrhiza_biostimulants/Begum-et-al-2019-amf-growth-regulation-review.pdf`

### 3. Biostimulant categories and microbial inoculant logic
- Definitions and scope: what counts as a biostimulant versus fertilizer or direct pest control.
- Categories covered locally: microbial inoculants, humic acids, fulvic acids, protein hydrolysates/amino acids, seaweed extracts.
- Microbial inoculant mechanisms: nutrient mobilization, hormone signaling, drought and salinity tolerance, co-inoculation effects, formulation and field-consistency constraints.
- Primary source: `knowledgebase/07_fungi_mycorrhiza_biostimulants/duJardin-2014-agricultural-uses-of-biostimulants.pdf`

### 4. Rhizosphere conflict ecology
- Pathogen-beneficial interactions in the root zone.
- Biocontrol mechanisms: competition, antibiosis, lytic enzymes, type III secretion effects, hyperparasitism.
- Plant-mediated defense: induced systemic resistance, jasmonate/ethylene-linked responses, some salicylate cases.
- Resistance/counter-resistance: pathogen tolerance to antimicrobials, efflux, degradation, quorum interference.
- Primary source: `knowledgebase/07_fungi_mycorrhiza_biostimulants/Raaijmakers-et-al-2008-rhizosphere-playground-and-battlefield.pdf`

### 5. Soil suppressiveness and management levers
- Agricultural practices that shift pathogen or antagonist populations.
- Organic amendments, biofumigation, suppressive soils, colonization ecology, inoculum dynamics.
- Primary source: `knowledgebase/07_fungi_mycorrhiza_biostimulants/Raaijmakers-et-al-2008-rhizosphere-playground-and-battlefield.pdf`

## Unresolved Gaps and Weak Evidence Areas

- Combined-stress evidence is limited in the local AMF review; use cautious language for stacked stresses instead of presenting a settled playbook.
- The biostimulant review shows broad promise but repeatedly implies variability by crop, cultivar, soil, formulation, and field conditions; do not overgeneralize greenhouse or hydroponic results.
- The local source set is strong on mechanisms and broad reviews, but weak on crop-specific, region-specific, and on-farm application protocols.
- The local source set does not provide a dedicated, current inoculant product-comparison framework; avoid recommending brands or exact commercial programs from this folder alone.
- Rhizosphere biocontrol mechanisms are well described, but real-world outcomes depend on colonization success, pathogen adaptation, and management context; flag this when asked for certainty.
