## Unresolved Gaps / Weak Evidence

- The folder is strong on ecological mechanisms and reviews, but weak on crop-specific vegetable protocols for Mid-Atlantic home-scale gardens.
- `references/crop-pest-mitigation-matrix.md` partially closes the crop-specific mapping gap, but it still relies on outside extension/IPM sources for several crop groups and should be refreshed if the local inventories expand substantially.
- Wildflower intervention evidence is strongest in fruit-crop contexts; transfer to mixed annual vegetable beds needs caution.
- The folder supports interpreting pests as system signals, but it does not provide rigorous diagnostic thresholds for when nutrient imbalance vs habitat deficit is the dominant cause.
- There is limited direct local evidence on slug-specific management, brassica flea beetles, and region-specific phenology in this folder alone.
- Flower-strip literature shows context dependence; not all strips improve in-crop control, and poorly chosen species can support pests or fail to spill over.
- Microbe and AMF papers are mechanistically strong but not enough to promise field-level suppression without broader soil-management context.
