# Bokashi & EM Retrieval Taxonomy

Use this file to route bokashi / EM / LAB questions to the fastest relevant local source before reading deeply.

## Major Themes

### 0. Bokashi step sequence and operating logic
- Scope: full step order, why each step exists, inoculated bran logic, layering, compaction, moisture and oxygen management, sealing, fermentation timing, and post-fermentation routing into soil, compost, or worms.
- Start with:
  `references/bokashi-sequence.md`
- Best for:
  "how do I actually do bokashi?", "what order should I do things in?", "why press it down?", "why seal it?", "what do I do after the bucket phase?", "how wet should it be?"

### 1. Bokashi systems and waste handling
- Scope: what bokashi is, how EM-bokashi is framed, fermentation time, feedstocks, storage, odor, farm waste integration, silage linkage.
- Start with:
  `knowledgebase/04_bokashi_em/Chavez-Rico-et-al-2023-bokashi-composting-waste-management.pdf`
- Best for:
  definitions, process framing, agricultural waste management, practical handling burden, environmental framing.

### 2. Bokashi agronomy and nursery economics
- Scope: bokashi as a soil amendment, nursery performance, nutrient efficiency, water retention, labor/cost tradeoffs, citrus greenhouse use, bokashi plus biochar.
- Start with:
  `knowledgebase/04_bokashi_em/Chavez-Rico-et-al-2024-bokashi-biochar-citrus.pdf`
- Best for:
  "does bokashi improve growth?", "is it cost-effective?", "how does it compare with fertilizer-heavy mixes?", "what about bokashi + biochar?"

### 3. LAB functional mechanisms
- Scope: LAB biology, plant colonization, direct nutrient effects, antimicrobial metabolites, induced resistance, plant-growth stimulation, limits of LAB as biocontrol agents.
- Start with:
  `knowledgebase/04_bokashi_em/Abdelrahman-et-al-2023-lactic-acid-bacteria-mini-review.pdf`
- Best for:
  mechanism questions, disease-suppression claims, plant-growth claims, root effects, plausibility of EM/LAB claims.

### 4. Microbial inoculants beyond bokashi
- Scope: broader biofertilizer framing, nutrient-fixing / solubilizing / mobilizing microbes, PGPR-style comparisons, inoculant limitations.
- Start with:
  `knowledgebase/04_bokashi_em/Fasusi-et-al-2021-microbes-as-biofertilizers.pdf`
- Best for:
  comparing bokashi/EM against other microbial inoculants, nutrient-cycling mechanisms beyond LAB, broader biofertilizer caveats.

## Weak Evidence / Gaps

- Local bokashi evidence is strongest for waste handling and one citrus nursery amendment study, not broad multi-crop field validation.
- Disease-control support is mostly indirect through LAB review mechanisms; the local folder does not contain a strong bokashi-specific plant disease field trial.
- EM consortium claims are heterogeneous. The local sources support functional possibilities more than a single standardized inoculant outcome.
- Application-rate, feedstock-specific, and climate-specific guidance is thin in this folder; broader system recommendations should usually hand off to soil fertility or regional adaptation experts.
- Long-term soil ecology and repeated-use outcomes are weaker locally than short-term process or nursery results.
- Brand-specific EM claims are not well validated here; answer from mechanisms and trial context, not from proprietary marketing language.
