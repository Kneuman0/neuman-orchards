## Question Routing Patterns

- "Can a microfarm actually make a living on a very small area?"
  - Open `Bec-Hellouin-Farm-Profitability-study.pdf` first for observed 1,000 m2 case data.
  - Then open `Morel-2017-small-can-be-beautiful.pdf` for model-based viability conditions across French microfarms.
  - Use `Morel-2016-microfarm-thesis.pdf` if the question expands into broader viability logic or farm diversity.

- "How much labor does an intensive permaculture market garden require?"
  - Open `Bec-Hellouin-Farm-Profitability-study.pdf` first for recorded working time, productivity shifts, and ergonomic reconfiguration effects.
  - Then open `Morel-2017-small-can-be-beautiful.pdf` for workload assumptions across crops and scenarios.

- "Is the Bec Hellouin model transferable, or is it just one special case?"
  - Open `Bec-Hellouin-Farm-Profitability-study.pdf` for the case itself.
  - Then open `Morel-2017-small-can-be-beautiful.pdf` and `Morel-2016-microfarm-thesis.pdf` to test generalization beyond one site.
  - Flag climate-transfer questions for the Mid-Atlantic expert.

- "What does permaculture add beyond biointensive gardening?"
  - Open `Bec-Hellouin-Farm-Profitability-study.pdf` for its explicit combination of permaculture spatial organization with biointensive methods.
  - Then open `Krebs-Bach-2018-permaculture-scientific-evidence.pdf` for principle-level scientific grounding.
  - Use `Morel-2016-microfarm-thesis.pdf` for the historical and conceptual sections on permaculture, biointensive, and their hybridization.

- "How should I design a small ecological market garden as a whole system?"
  - Open `Morel-2016-microfarm-thesis.pdf` first for system framing and hybrid inspirations.
  - Then open `Bec-Hellouin-Farm-Profitability-study.pdf` for operational consequences of intensive-core design.
  - If the question asks for evidence behind the design principles, add `Krebs-Bach-2018-permaculture-scientific-evidence.pdf`.

- "Why do different microfarms make different choices even when they share ecological values?"
  - Open `Morel-Leger-2016-conceptual-framework.md` first.
  - Then open the strategic-choice sections in `Morel-2016-microfarm-thesis.pdf`.

- "Which permaculture principles are scientifically supported, and which are still weakly evidenced?"
  - Open `Krebs-Bach-2018-permaculture-scientific-evidence.pdf` first.
  - Use `Morel-2016-microfarm-thesis.pdf` only to connect evidence discussion back to working microfarm practice.

- "What are the main constraints or failure points in microfarm viability?"
  - Open `Morel-2017-small-can-be-beautiful.pdf` first for modeled viability thresholds and scenario sensitivity.
  - Then open `Bec-Hellouin-Farm-Profitability-study.pdf` for observed constraints: workload, skill, equipment, market fit, and seasonality.
  - Use `Morel-Leger-2016-conceptual-framework.md` if the question is really about tradeoffs rather than arithmetic.

## Weak Evidence / Unresolved Gaps

- Bec Hellouin is a strong anchor but still a single intensive-case study; do not generalize its income figures without checking `Morel-2017-small-can-be-beautiful.pdf` or the thesis.
- The local conceptual-framework source is only a short markdown note plus a link, so detailed argument reconstruction may require the thesis instead.
- Transferability to the user's climate, soils, and market context is weakly resolved inside this folder alone; hand off climate-fit questions to the Mid-Atlantic expert.
- This folder is stronger on viability framing, labor realism, and design logic than on exact crop scheduling, rotations, pest pressure, or fertility protocols; hand those to the relevant specialists.
- `Krebs-Bach-2018-permaculture-scientific-evidence.pdf` strengthens principle-level justification, but it does not by itself prove that any named microfarm blueprint will be economically viable in a new context.
