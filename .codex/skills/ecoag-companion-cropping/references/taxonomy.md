## 1. Major Themes

### A. General intercropping logic, mixture design, and performance framing
- System-level reasons to mix species: productivity, resilience, pest buffering, ecological services, profitability.
- Design logic: competition vs facilitation, aboveground vs belowground interactions, row vs mixed arrangements, modeling limits.
- Best first sources:
  `G:\My Drive\Home Docs\Garden\knowledgebase\10_companion_planting_intercropping\Malezieux-et-al-2009-mixing-plant-species-review.pdf`

### B. Yield advantage, resource-use efficiency, and intercrop evaluation
- Broad intercropping benefits under low-input management.
- Metrics and management framing for combined yield, land-use advantage, and competition control.
- Best first source:
  `G:\My Drive\Home Docs\Garden\knowledgebase\10_companion_planting_intercropping\Maitra-et-al-2021-intercropping-low-input-strategy-review.pdf`

### C. Legume companioning and belowground nitrogen transfer
- When legumes may feed neighboring non-legumes, and by which route.
- Mechanisms: decomposition of roots/nodules, root exudates, mycorrhiza-mediated transfer.
- Moderators: water stress, temperature, soil N, fertilizer, root contact, density, growth stage, defoliation, root herbivory.
- Best first source:
  `G:\My Drive\Home Docs\Garden\knowledgebase\10_companion_planting_intercropping\Thilakarathna-et-al-2016-belowground-nitrogen-transfer-review.pdf`

### D. Pest management with companion plants: trap, repellent, enemy-support
- Trap cropping for diverting pests away from the main crop.
- Companion-plant mechanisms for pest disruption, attraction, and support of natural enemies.
- Best first sources:
  `G:\My Drive\Home Docs\Garden\knowledgebase\10_companion_planting_intercropping\Sarkar-et-al-2018-trap-cropping-companion-plants-review.pdf`
  `G:\My Drive\Home Docs\Garden\knowledgebase\10_companion_planting_intercropping\Ben-Issa-et-al-2017-companion-plants-aphid-pest-management.pdf`

### E. Maize-centered mixed systems, milpa, and push-pull
- Maize-bean-squash logic, push-pull logic, and combined food-plus-pest-control system design.
- Especially relevant for maize, stemborers, fall armyworm, Striga, and companion-plant role differentiation.
- Best first source:
  `G:\My Drive\Home Docs\Garden\knowledgebase\10_companion_planting_intercropping\Libran-Embid-et-al-2023-milpa-push-pull-review.pdf`

## 5. Unresolved Gaps / Weak Evidence In This Folder

- Most local sources are reviews, so this folder is stronger for mechanisms, design logic, and evidence summaries than for direct local field prescriptions.
- Companion-planting claims are highly mechanism-specific. Evidence is much stronger here for aphids, trap cropping, maize push-pull, and legume N transfer than for generic "good neighbor" folklore.
- Nitrogen transfer evidence is real but conditional; transfer depends heavily on species pairing, density, root contact, water, soil N, and growth stage, so this folder does not support blanket claims that legumes always feed nearby crops.
- Trap-cropping evidence is promising but the Sarkar review explicitly notes little consensus on an optimal trap-cropping system across situations.
- The aphid paper supports multiple mechanisms, but also stresses that successful companion planting depends on many contextual factors and usually should be combined with other approaches.
- Milpa plus push-pull is compelling conceptually in the 2023 review, but cross-system learning remains limited, so adaptation outside maize-centered systems should be framed cautiously.
