# Ecological Agriculture Knowledge Base

This folder was built to support a future Codex skill on ecological agriculture, with Bec Hellouin as the anchor paper and a curated set of peer-reviewed papers or stable link records around small-scale agroecological production, composting, microbes, forest gardens, companion planting, edible wild-plant integration, and region-relevant Mid-Atlantic references.

## Notes on capture method

- Most entries below are local PDFs saved into topic folders.
- A smaller set are saved as `.url` shortcuts plus local `.md` notes because direct PDF capture was blocked by publisher anti-bot pages from this environment.
- `catalog.csv` is the machine-readable index for the paper set.
- `Bec_Hellouin_summary.md` is the local study summary.
- `13_inventories_and_local_lists` contains the copied perennial workbook, CSV exports of its sheets, and a separate self-seeding annuals-and-short-lived list.
- `12_beneficial_insects_pest_indicators` also contains a synthesis note on beneficial plants, beneficial insects, and reading pest pressure as a management signal.

## Bec Hellouin anchor study

- Intensive nucleus studied: 1,000 m2, not the whole farm.
- Protected cultivation share: about 42%.
- Recorded themes: hand-scale intensification, intercropping, season extension, ergonomics, labor, and local-market fit.
- Main caution: the intensive core depends on the wider ecological and commercial whole-farm system.

## Catalog by folder

### 01_core_microfarm_permaculture

- `Bec-Hellouin-Farm-Profitability-study.pdf` - Permacultural Organic Market Gardening and Economic Performance (2015). Local anchor study. Real-world economic case study of the Bec Hellouin intensive permaculture market-garden nucleus.
- `Morel-2016-microfarm-thesis.pdf` - These kevin morel microfermes 2016 (2016). Long-form dissertation covering French organic microfarms, their design, work organization, and viability logic beyond the single Bec Hellouin report.
- `Morel-2017-small-can-be-beautiful.pdf` - Small can be beautiful for organic market gardens: an exploration of the economic viability of French microfarms using MERLIN (2017). Direct follow-on economic modeling of microfarm viability, useful for turning Bec Hellouin-style ideas into decision rules.
- `Krebs-Bach-2018-permaculture-scientific-evidence.pdf` - Permaculture-Scientific Evidence of Principles for the Agroecological Design of Farming Systems (2018). Bridges permaculture claims to peer-reviewed agroecological evidence, which is important for a future Codex skill.
- `Morel-Leger-2016-conceptual-framework.url` - A conceptual framework for alternative farmers' strategic choices: the case of French organic market gardening microfarms (2016). Key conceptual paper on how alternative microfarmers balance ecological, social, and economic tradeoffs. Saved as link because direct PDF export was blocked.

### 02_intensive_vegetable_systems

- `Felix-et-al-2023-agroecological-microfarm-prototype.pdf` - Co-Design and Experimentation of a Prototype of Agroecological Micro-Farm Meeting the Objectives Set by Climate-Smart Agriculture (2023). Recent experimental microfarm paper with explicit design criteria, useful for the technical side of small-scale agroecological farm planning.
- `Norris-Congreves-2018-intensive-vegetable-soil-health-review.pdf` - Alternative Management Practices Improve Soil Health Indices in Intensive Vegetable Cropping Systems: A Review (2018). High-value review for intensive vegetable production: amendments, cover crops, reduced tillage, soil biology, soil chemistry, and yield tradeoffs.
- `Navarrete-Dupre-Lamine-2014-crop-management-labour-marketing.url` - Crop management, labour organization, and marketing: three key issues for improving sustainability in organic vegetable farming (2014). Important market-garden systems paper on diversification, labor, and marketing. Saved as link because the direct PDF host blocked download.
- `Poeplau-et-al-2017-cover-crops-climate-review.pdf` - Using cover crops to mitigate and adapt to climate change. A review (2017). Cover crops are foundational to ecological farming; this review supports crop-rotation, soil protection, and adaptive management decisions.

### 03_biochar

- `Vera-Ruiz-et-al-2020-use-of-biochar-in-agriculture.pdf` - Use of Biochar in agriculture (2020). Broad biochar reference for soil amendment effects, mechanisms, and agricultural use.
- `Kammann-et-al-2015-co-composted-biochar.pdf` - Plant growth improvement mediated by nitrate capture in co-composted biochar (2015). Directly relevant to integrating biochar with composting rather than treating it as an isolated input.
- `Jaiswal-et-al-2017-biochar-tomato-disease-suppression.pdf` - Linking the Belowground Microbial Composition, Diversity and Activity to Soilborne Disease Suppression and Growth Promotion of Tomato Amended with Biochar (2017). Connects biochar to microbiome shifts, disease suppression, and vegetable crop performance.
- `Singh-et-al-2025-cucumber-biochar-deficit-irrigation.pdf` - Influence of deficit irrigation and biochar amendment on growth, physiology, and yield of cucumber in West Texas (2025). Recent crop-level paper showing how biochar integrates with irrigation management in practical vegetable production.

### 04_bokashi_em

- `Chavez-Rico-et-al-2024-bokashi-biochar-citrus.pdf` - Recycling Agricultural Waste to Enhance Sustainable Greenhouse Agriculture: Analyzing the Cost-Effectiveness and Agronomic Benefits of Bokashi and Biochar Byproducts as Soil Amendments in Citrus Nursery Production (2024). Useful modern bokashi paper because it evaluates agronomic and cost dimensions together.
- `Chavez-Rico-et-al-2023-bokashi-composting-waste-management.pdf` - Utilization of Bokashi Composting and Animal Feed Silage for Sustainable Agricultural Waste Management and Environmental Impact Analysis (2023). Direct bokashi systems paper on fermentation-based waste handling and farm nutrient cycling.
- `Fasusi-et-al-2021-microbes-as-biofertilizers.pdf` - Microbes as Biofertilizers, a Potential Approach for Sustainable Crop Production (2021). Broad microbial inoculant reference that supports EM-style thinking with a wider scientific base.
- `Abdelrahman-et-al-2023-lactic-acid-bacteria-mini-review.pdf` - The potential of lactic acid bacteria in mediating the control of plant diseases and plant growth stimulation in crop production - A mini review (2023). Relevant to bokashi fermentation because lactic acid bacteria are a core functional group in those systems.

### 05_compost_soil_fertility

- `Awasthi-et-al-2020-waste-management-through-composting.pdf` - Waste Management through Composting: Challenges and Potentials (2020). Composting foundation paper on process control, constraints, and scaling limits.
- `Abakari-et-al-2023-thermophilic-bacteria-composting-review.pdf` - Thermophilic bacteria and their thermozymes in composting processes: a review (2023). Useful for process-level compost science: heat, decomposition, thermophiles, and microbial function.

### 06_compost_tea_soil_biology

- `Pane-et-al-2025-compost-tea-review.pdf` - Sustainable Agriculture Through Compost Tea: Production, Application, and Impact on Horticultural Crops (2025). Dedicated compost tea review focused on production methods, application logic, and horticultural outcomes.
- `Bashan-et-al-2013-pgpr-inoculant-technology.pdf` - Advances in plant growth-promoting bacterial inoculant technology: formulations and practical perspectives (1998-2013) (2013). Practical inoculant paper for turning microbial theory into deployment choices.
- `Jacoby-et-al-2017-soil-microorganisms-plant-nutrition.pdf` - The Role of Soil Microorganisms in Plant Mineral Nutrition-Current Knowledge and Future Directions (2017). Soil biology backbone paper on nutrient mobilization, uptake, and microbe-mediated fertility.

### 07_fungi_mycorrhiza_biostimulants

- `Begum-et-al-2019-amf-growth-regulation-review.pdf` - Role of Arbuscular Mycorrhizal Fungi in Plant Growth Regulation: Implications in Abiotic Stress Tolerance (2019). Core review on mycorrhizae, stress tolerance, and plant performance.
- `duJardin-2014-agricultural-uses-of-biostimulants.pdf` - Agricultural uses of plant biostimulants (2014). Important framework paper for inoculants, extracts, humic substances, and related biological amendments.
- `Raaijmakers-et-al-2008-rhizosphere-playground-and-battlefield.pdf` - The rhizosphere: a playground and battlefield for soilborne pathogens and beneficial microorganisms (2008). Foundational rhizosphere paper for disease suppression, antagonism, and beneficial microbe ecology.

### 08_forest_gardens_agroforestry

- `Castro-et-al-2018-temperate-agroforestry-forest-gardens.pdf` - Temperate Agroforestry: How Forest Garden Systems Combined with People-Based Ethics Can Transform Culture (2018). Direct forest-garden paper and the clearest bridge from permaculture language to temperate agroforestry language.
- `Castro-et-al-2025-temperate-food-forests-scoping-review.pdf` - A systematic scoping literature review into temperate food forests: an overview of the current knowledge and a research agenda (2025). Recent state-of-the-literature review specifically on temperate food forests.
- `Galhena-et-al-2013-home-gardens-food-security.pdf` - Home gardens: a promising approach to enhance household food security and wellbeing (2013). Home-garden review is useful because many forest-garden systems borrow the same multistrata logic.
- `Jose-2019-agroforestry-and-biodiversity.pdf` - Agroforestry and Biodiversity (2019). Broad agroforestry systems review that helps justify forest-garden multifunctionality.
- `Ortiz-et-al-2020-forests-agroforestry-giahs-review.pdf` - A Review of the Role of Forests and Agroforestry Systems in the FAO Globally Important Agricultural Heritage Systems Programme (2020). Useful systems-level review on how perennial multistrata systems function across heritage farming contexts.
- `Bishop-et-al-2023-eastern-black-walnut-alley-cropping-review.pdf` - A review to frame the utilization of Eastern black walnut (Juglans nigra L.) cultivars in alley cropping systems (2023). Climate-relevant agroforestry review for eastern North American tree-crop systems.

### 09_mid_atlantic_priority

- `Horrigan-Lawrence-Walker-2002-sustainable-agriculture-harms.url` - How sustainable agriculture can address the environmental and human health harms of industrial agriculture (2002). Baltimore-based review that frames why agroecological redesign matters at all. Saved as link because PMC blocked direct PDF export here.
- `Cavigelli-et-al-2009-mid-atlantic-organic-economics.url` - Long-term economic performance of organic and conventional field crops in the mid-Atlantic region (2009). Beltsville / University of Maryland long-term economics paper. Saved as link because direct PDF export was blocked.
- `Thapa-et-al-2018-hairy-vetch-cereal-rye-meta-analysis.pdf` - Biomass Production and Nitrogen Accumulation by Hairy Vetch-Cereal Rye Mixtures: A Meta-Analysis (2018). University of Maryland / Beltsville-linked cover-crop meta-analysis, directly useful for your climate and soil-building plans.
- `Mirsky-et-al-2013-organic-rotational-no-till-soybean.url` - Overcoming Weed Management Challenges in Cover Crop-Based Organic Rotational No-Till Soybean Production in the Eastern United States (2013). Eastern U.S. no-till / cover-crop systems paper relevant to reduced tillage ecological production. Saved as stable DOI link.

### 10_companion_planting_intercropping

- `Malezieux-et-al-2009-mixing-plant-species-review.pdf` - Mixing plant species in cropping systems: concepts, tools and models. A review (2009). Foundational review on species mixtures, system design logic, and modeling for diversified cropping.
- `Sarkar-et-al-2018-trap-cropping-companion-plants-review.pdf` - Application of Trap Cropping as Companion Plants for the Management of Agricultural Pests: A Review (2018). Direct companion-planting pest-management review with practical trap-crop mechanisms and case summaries.
- `Thilakarathna-et-al-2016-belowground-nitrogen-transfer-review.pdf` - Belowground nitrogen transfer from legumes to non-legumes under managed herbaceous cropping systems. A review (2016). Useful for legume companion planting, relay cropping, and mixed-stand fertility design.
- `Libran-Embid-et-al-2023-milpa-push-pull-review.pdf` - Combining Milpa and Push-Pull Technology for sustainable food production in smallholder agriculture. A review (2023). Strong systems paper on companion cropping for fertility, pest management, and resilient production.
- `Ben-Issa-et-al-2017-companion-plants-aphid-pest-management.pdf` - Companion Plants for Aphid Pest Management (2017). Focused review on a concrete companion-plant mechanism: aphid disruption, attraction, and biological control support.
- `Maitra-et-al-2021-intercropping-low-input-strategy-review.pdf` - Intercropping-A Low Input Agricultural Strategy for Food and Environmental Security (2021). Broad synthesis of intercropping benefits, constraints, metrics, and ecological mechanisms.

### 11_wild_plants_foraged_integration

- `Pardo-de-Santayana-et-al-2007-wild-edible-plants-iberian-peninsula.pdf` - Traditional knowledge of wild edible plants used in the northwest of the Iberian Peninsula (Spain and Portugal): a comparative study (2007). Strong ethnobotanical baseline on wild edible species, edible parts, and knowledge systems around gathered foods.
- `Stark-et-al-2019-open-source-food-wild-edible-greens.pdf` - Open-source food: Nutrition, toxicology, and availability of wild edible greens in the East Bay (2019). Useful for edible weeds as a managed resource: nutrient density, toxicology, and abundance in disturbed urban ecologies.
- `Hong-Zimmerer-2022-useful-plants-wild-to-home-gardens.url` - Useful Plants from the Wild to Home Gardens: An Analysis of Home Garden Ethnobotany in Contexts of Habitat Conversion and Land Use Change in Jeju, South Korea (2022). Most direct paper in the set on moving useful wild plants into managed home-garden systems.
- `Russo-et-al-2017-edible-green-infrastructure-review.url` - Edible green infrastructure: An approach and review of provisioning ecosystem services and disservices in urban environments (2017). Review on incorporating edible plants into designed landscapes while accounting for provisioning services, contamination, and tradeoffs.
- `Polyzos-et-al-2025-wild-edible-plants-valorization-review.pdf` - The new trends in wild edible plants valorization: commercial cultivation protocols, agronomic practices and future challenges (2025). Direct cultivation-focused review on domesticating and managing wild edible species in farming systems.
- `Kumar-et-al-2025-wild-edible-plants-global-overview.pdf` - Wild edible plants for food security, dietary diversity, and nutraceuticals: a global overview of emerging research (2025). Recent overview connecting wild edible plants to food security, local food systems, domestication, and sustainable harvesting.
- `Luczaj-et-al-2012-wild-food-plant-use-in-21st-century-europe.url` - Wild food plant use in 21st century Europe: the disappearance of old traditions and the search for new cuisines involving wild edibles (2012). Useful cultural and culinary context for integrating foraged plants into modern food systems, kitchens, and gardens.

### 12_beneficial_insects_pest_indicators

- `Landis-Wratten-Gurr-2000-habitat-management-natural-enemies.url` - Habitat Management to Conserve Natural Enemies of Arthropod Pests in Agriculture (2000). Foundational review on habitat management, insectary resources, and conservation biological control.
- `Ratnadass-et-al-2011-plant-species-diversity-pests-diseases-review.pdf` - Plant species diversity for sustainable management of crop pests and diseases in agroecosystems: a review (2011). Best broad review in the set for why plant diversity can suppress pests through resource dilution, natural enemies, microclimate, and improved crop physiology.
- `Pearsons-Tooker-2017-in-field-habitat-management-soil-communities.pdf` - In-Field Habitat Management to Optimize Pest Control of Novel Soil Communities in Agroecosystems (2017). Directly relevant to cover crops, reduced tillage, soil biodiversity, and predator support for belowground and surface-active pest control.
- `Kowalska-et-al-2022-flower-strips-ecological-multifunctionality.pdf` - Flower Strips and Their Ecological Multifunctionality in Agricultural Fields (2022). Practical review on flower strips as beneficial-insect infrastructure and on the tradeoffs around strip design and management.
- `Fountain-2022-wildflower-interventions-beneficial-insects-review.pdf` - Impacts of Wildflower Interventions on Beneficial Insects in Fruit Crops: A Review (2022). Useful review on which wildflower interventions actually help beneficial insects and how placement and management matter.
- `Rashid-Chung-2017-systemic-resistance-insect-herbivores-beneficial-microbes.pdf` - Induction of Systemic Resistance against Insect Herbivores in Plants by Beneficial Soil Microbes (2017). Important review for the idea that healthy rhizosphere biology can improve direct plant resistance to herbivores, not just nutrition.
- `Tripathi-et-al-2022-plant-mineral-nutrition-disease-resistance.pdf` - Plant mineral nutrition and disease resistance: A significant linkage for sustainable crop protection (2022). Best fit for using disease and disorder pressure as a nutrient-balance diagnostic rather than treating all problems as spray problems.
- `Stratton-et-al-2022-amf-association-plant-defenses.pdf` - Nutrition vs association: plant defenses are altered by arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi association not by nutritional provisioning alone (2022). Useful mechanistic paper showing that mycorrhiza-driven defense effects are not explained by nutrition alone.
- `Singh-Sood-2017-plant-nutrition-hemipteran-insect-pests-review.pdf` - Plant Nutrition: A tool for the management of hemipteran insect-pests-A review (2017). Direct review on how nutrient balance shifts pressure from sap-feeding pests such as aphids, leafhoppers, whiteflies, and related groups.
- `beneficial_plants_bugs_and_pest_indicators.md` - local synthesis note linking the papers to practical pest-diagnostic and insectary design choices.
