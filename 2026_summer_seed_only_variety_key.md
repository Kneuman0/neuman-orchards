## Working Rule

- Annual crop recommendations in this workspace assume `direct-sown outdoor seed only`.
- If a crop is poorly suited to that constraint at the current timing, it should be replaced rather than shifted to indoor starts or bought transplants.

## Bed 1: Trellised Cucumbers

### Best disease-focused choices

- `Bristol`
  - Why: strong disease package in the current Mid-Atlantic cucumber recommendations, including downy mildew resistance.
  - Fit: good if you want the strongest extension-backed slicing cucumber from seed.

- `Mongoose`
  - Why: also carries a broad disease package and fits the same direct-sown trellised slicing role.
  - Fit: good alternate if `Bristol` is unavailable.

- `Stonewall`
  - Why: early and broadly resistant in the current UMD commercial recommendations.
  - Fit: good backup if the first two are not available.

### Easier common fallback

- `Marketmore 76`
  - Why: standard slicing type, widely available, monoecious, and still present in UMD recommendations.
  - Fit: use this if the stronger hybrid disease-resistant options are hard to source locally.

### Direct-sow note

- Sow after frost danger and warm soil, then thin to about `9-12 inches`.
- If you use gynoecious seed, the seed packet may include pollinizer seed. That is normal.

## Bed 2: Pole Beans

### Primary choices

- `Kentucky Blue`
  - Why: good home-garden track record, direct-sow friendly, and Cornell lists resistance to bean common mosaic virus and rust.
  - Fit: best all-around pole bean for this plan if you want a standard green snap bean.

- `Blue Lake Pole`
  - Why: classic pole snap bean, strong eating quality, and Cornell lists bean common mosaic resistance.
  - Fit: best backup if `Kentucky Blue` is not available.

### Other good options

- `Kentucky Wonder 191`
  - Why: widely available, strong flavor, and Cornell lists bean common mosaic virus and rust resistance.
  - Fit: easiest traditional fallback.

- `Rattlesnake`
  - Why: useful if you want an open-pollinated/heirloom option; Cornell lists bean common mosaic resistance.
  - Fit: fine choice if you save seed or prefer heirlooms.

## Bed 3 Front 10 Feet: Bush Snap Beans

### Primary choices

- `Provider`
  - Why: widely available and Cornell lists a strong disease package including bean common mosaic virus, downy mildew, powdery mildew, pod mottle virus, and rust.
  - Fit: best practical first choice for the weak bed because it balances sourcing ease with resilience.

- `Jade II`
  - Why: UMD and Cornell both show good resistance coverage; strong choice if you can find it.
  - Fit: best disease-focused upgrade over `Provider`.

### Stronger-resistance but less-common options

- `Crockett`
  - Why: very broad resistance package in both Cornell and UMD listings.
  - Fit: excellent if seed is available, but less likely to be on a common seed rack.

- `Caprice`
  - Why: broad resistance and good fit for Mid-Atlantic recommendations.
  - Fit: another good upgraded option if sourcing is not a problem.

## Support Plants

### Dill

- `Bouquet`
  - Why: compact and easy; good default for bed-edge sowing.

- `Fernleaf`
  - Why: dwarf habit and slower to bolt.

- `Green Sleeves`
  - Why: better bolting resistance if you want a longer dill leaf window.

### Cilantro

- `Santo`
  - Why: slow bolting and good flavor.

- `Leisure`
  - Why: high yielding, heat tolerant, and slow to bolt.

### Basil

- `Genovese`
  - Why: standard culinary choice and easiest default.

- `Emily`
  - Why: more compact if you want basil in smaller spaces.

- `Dolly`
  - Why: heavier large-leaf production and somewhat more cold tolerant than standard Genovese-group basil.

## Recovery Cover In Bed 3

- `Common buckwheat`
  - Why: for this use, the species matters more than the cultivar. UMD recommends buckwheat as a spring/summer cover crop that grows quickly.
  - Fit: use ordinary buckwheat seed sold for cover-crop use.

## Simple Buying Priority

If you want the shortest shopping list:

1. `Bristol` cucumber, or `Marketmore 76` if that is what you can actually get
2. `Kentucky Blue` pole bean, or `Blue Lake Pole`
3. `Provider` bush bean
4. `Bouquet` dill
5. `Santo` cilantro
6. `Genovese` basil
7. `Common buckwheat`
